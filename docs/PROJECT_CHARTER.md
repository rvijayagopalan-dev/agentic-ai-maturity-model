## 1. Executive Summary

This charter formally authorises the Enterprise AI Evolution & Maturity Program — a 38-month strategic initiative to progressively transform Customer Support & Service Operations through AI capabilities, evolving from basic prompt-based AI (Level 1) to an Autonomous Enterprise (Level 10). The program delivers a projected 3-year NPV of $14.2M with a payback period of 14 months.

---

## 2. Business Justification

| Driver | Description |
|---|---|
| Cost pressure | Support costs growing 8% YoY; headcount not scalable at current trajectory |
| Customer experience | 18% below industry CSAT benchmark; 24-hour average resolution time |
| Competitive threat | Key competitors investing heavily in AI-powered support |
| Regulatory requirement | Increasing compliance burden requires consistent, auditable responses |
| Strategic alignment | Supports 5-year digital transformation and AI-first strategy |

---

## 3. Program Objectives

1. Achieve 75% AI containment rate for customer support interactions by Year 3
2. Reduce average resolution time from 24 hours to 4 hours
3. Increase CSAT from 3.6 to 4.4 out of 5
4. Reduce cost-per-case from $90 to $22.50
5. Enable 24/7 global support coverage without proportional headcount growth
6. Establish enterprise AI governance capability and AI Centre of Excellence

---

## 4. Scope

### In Scope
- AI platform build (LLM Gateway, RAG, Agents, Workflows, Tool Registry, Memory)
- Customer Support & Service Operations domain (10 maturity levels)
- Integrations: CRM, ERP, Payment Gateway, Logistics, Identity
- Security, observability, DevOps, governance, change management

### Out of Scope
- AI for other business domains (Finance, HR, Sales) — Phase 2 program
- CRM/ERP system replacement
- Consumer mobile app development
- Legacy IVR system replacement
- Model fine-tuning (Phase 1 feasibility only)

**Steering Committee Cadence:** Monthly; ad-hoc for go/no-go decisions

### AI Review Board
- Approves model deployments, bias audits, AI policy updates
- Chaired by Chief AI Officer
- Monthly cadence; < 5-day turnaround for urgent reviews

### Phase Gate Process
- Each phase requires: technical readiness + quality gate pass + security review + business UAT sign-off + financial approval
- Go/No-Go decision made by Steering Committee
- Red phases do not proceed without remediation plan approved

## 10. Risks & Constraints

**Top Risks:**
- Agent hallucination quality at scale (mitigated via HITL + RAG + eval)
- AI engineering talent availability (mitigated via SI partner + competitive hiring)
- Regulatory change (EU AI Act) impacting autonomous use cases

**Constraints:**
- No customer-facing disruption during rollout (canary deployments only)
- Agent redeployment policy: no redundancies from AI automation
- All AI actions must be auditable and explainable
